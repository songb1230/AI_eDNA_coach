🔬 Library Prep · Module 1
Step 4 · DNA Repair & End-Prep
Repairs damage, adds dA-tails, and cleans up with AMPure XP beads. ~35 min.
💡
Tip Use wide-bore pipette tips for long gDNA fragments to preserve DNA length during mixing.
⚠️
Important Do NOT vortex the FFPE DNA Repair Mix or Ultra II End Prep Enzyme Mix. Flick or invert only. Buffers may be vortexed.

Reagent Volumes per Sample (Reaction Master Mix)

ReagentVolume
DNA sample11 µl
Diluted DNA Control Sample (DCS)1 µl
NEBNext FFPE DNA Repair Buffer0.875 µl
Ultra II End-prep Reaction Buffer0.875 µl
Ultra II End-prep Enzyme Mix0.75 µl
NEBNext FFPE DNA Repair Mix0.5 µl
Total15 µl
💡
Tip Make a mastermix of the 4 enzyme/buffer reagents (3 µl total) for all samples and add to each well.

Protocol Steps

1
Thaw AMPure XP Beads (AXP) and DNA Control Sample (DCS) at room temperature. Vortex to mix. Keep beads at RT; store DCS on ice.
2
Prepare NEB reagents on ice: thaw on ice, flick/invert to mix (do NOT vortex enzyme mixes). Vortex buffers 30 sec to dissolve any precipitate.
3
Dilute DCS: add 105 µl Elution Buffer (EB) to one DCS tube. Mix gently by pipetting, spin down. (Good for 140 samples; store excess at -20°C.)
4
In clean 0.2 ml PCR tubes / 96-well plate, aliquot DNA: 400 ng per sample. Make up to 11 µl with nuclease-free water.
5
Add reagents per the table above (pipette mix 10–20× between each addition). Ensure thoroughly mixed, spin down.
6
Incubate in thermal cycler: 20°C for 5 min → 65°C for 5 min
Incubation timer
10:00
7
Transfer each sample to a clean 1.5 ml DNA LoBind tube.
8
Vortex AXP beads to resuspend. Add 15 µl AXP to each sample. Mix by flicking.
9
Incubate on Hula mixer for 5 min at RT.
Hula mixer
05:00
10
Prepare fresh 80% ethanol (~400 µl/sample + excess). Spin samples, pellet beads on magnet until eluate is clear. Remove supernatant.
11
Wash beads with 200 µl 80% ethanol (keep on magnet, do not disturb pellet). Remove and discard. Repeat once (2 washes total).
12
Briefly spin, return to magnet, remove residual ethanol. Air-dry ~30 seconds — do not crack the pellet!
13
Resuspend pellet in 10 µl nuclease-free water. Spin down, incubate 2 min at RT, then re-pellet on magnet.
14
Remove and retain 10 µl eluate into a clean LoBind tube. Discard beads.

🔗 Library Prep · Module 3
Step 6 · Adapter Ligation & Clean-up
Ligates the Native Adapter (NA) to pooled barcoded library, then cleans up with LFB or SFB. ~35 min.
⚠️
Critical The Native Adapter (NA) is NOT interchangeable with other sequencing adapters. Use only the NA supplied in this kit.
⚠️
Critical — Buffer Selection The wash step uses LFB or SFB — NOT ethanol! Ethanol denatures the motor protein on sequencing adapters.
• LFB (Long Fragment Buffer, orange cap) → enriches for fragments >3 kb
• SFB (Short Fragment Buffer, clear cap) → retains all fragment sizes

Ligation Reaction

ReagentVolume
Pooled barcoded sample30 µl
Native Adapter (NA)5 µl
NEBNext Quick Ligation Reaction Buffer (5X)10 µl
Quick T4 DNA Ligase5 µl
Total50 µl

Protocol Steps

1
Prepare NEBNext Quick Ligation Module on ice: thaw at RT, spin 5 sec, pipette mix 10×. Do NOT vortex Quick T4 DNA Ligase. Vortex buffer if precipitate visible.
2
Spin down Native Adapter (NA) and Quick T4 DNA Ligase. Pipette mix, place on ice.
3
Thaw chosen wash buffer (LFB or SFB) at RT, vortex, spin down, keep at RT. Also thaw Elution Buffer (EB), vortex, spin, place on ice.
4
In a 1.5 ml LoBind tube, add reagents per the table above (mix 10–20× between additions). Gently pipette mix, spin down briefly.
5
Incubate 20 min at RT.
Adapter ligation
20:00
6
Resuspend AXP by vortexing. Add 20 µl AXP. Mix by pipetting. Incubate on Hula mixer 10 min at RT.
Hula mixer
10:00

Pellet on magnetic rack. Remove supernatant. Wash with 125 µl LFB or SFB — flick to resuspend, spin, magnet, remove. Repeat once (2 washes).

Spin, magnet, remove residual supernatant. Resuspend in 15 µl Elution Buffer (EB).

🧪 Library Prep · Module 4
Step 7 · Priming & Loading the SpotON Flow Cell
Primes the R10.4.1 flow cell (FLO-MIN114) and loads 75 µl of DNA library. ~20 min.
⚠️
Critical Add BSA to priming mix at 0.2 mg/ml final for optimal output. Do not use recombinant human serum albumin.
🚨
Caution — Air Bubbles Never remove more than 20–30 µl when drawing back. Air bubbles in the nanopore array cause irreversible damage.

Priming Mix (per flow cell — Bottle Format)

ReagentVolume
Flow Cell Flush (FCF)1,170 µl
BSA (50 mg/ml)5 µl
Flow Cell Tether (FCT)30 µl
Total1,205 µl

Library Loading Mix (per flow cell)

ReagentVolume
Sequencing Buffer (SB)37.5 µl
Library Beads (LIB) — mix immediately before use25.5 µl
DNA library (12 µl in EB)12 µl
Total75 µl
⚠️
Library Beads LIB beads settle very quickly. Mix immediately before adding — do not pre-prepare.

Protocol Steps

1
Thaw SB, LIB (or LIS), FCT, FCF at RT. Vortex to mix, spin down, store on ice.
2
Prepare priming mix: combine FCF + BSA (5 µl) + FCT (30 µl) per the table above. Mix by pipetting.
3
Insert flow cell into MinION/GridION. Press firmly on priming port cover for thermal/electrical contact. Optionally run flow cell check now.
4
Slide priming port cover clockwise to open. Check for air bubble. Set P1000 to 200 µl, insert tip, turn wheel to 220–230 µl to draw back 20–30 µl, until buffer just enters tip.
5
Load 800 µl priming mix into priming port (not SpotON). Avoid air bubbles. Wait 5 min. During this time prepare your library loading mix.
Priming wait
05:00
6
Mix LIB beads thoroughly by pipetting immediately before use. Prepare loading mix per table above in a new 1.5 ml LoBind tube.
7
Gently lift SpotON sample port cover to expose SpotON port. Load 200 µl priming mix into the priming port (not SpotON). No air bubbles.
8
Pipette mix the library loading mix. Load 75 µl dropwise into the SpotON sample port — ensure each drop flows in before adding the next.
9
Gently replace SpotON cover (bung into port). Close priming port. Install light shield immediately.
🚨
Light Shield Install immediately after loading. Keep on during washing/reloading steps. The shield is not secured — handle carefully.

Step 10 · Flow Cell Reuse & Returns
Wash and store the flow cell for re-use, or return to Oxford Nanopore.
1
Wash the flow cell using the Flow Cell Wash Kit (EXP-WSH004) as soon as possible after the run. If not immediate, leave on device and wash the next day.
2
Store washed flow cell at +2°C to +8°C for re-use.
3
Alternatively, follow the returns procedure to send the flow cell back to Oxford Nanopore (see ONT Community for instructions).

Library Re-loading Libraries can be split and reloaded across multiple washed flow cells. Dilute in Elution Buffer (EB) if needed.

Troubleshooting Guide
Common issues, causes, and corrective actions.

Library Preparation Issues

⚠️ Low DNA purity (OD260/280 <1.8, OD260/230 <2.0–2.2)
Cause: Extraction method leaves chemical contaminants
→ Try alternative extraction method. Perform additional SPRI clean-up step. Avoid phenol-chloroform carryover.
⚠️ Low DNA recovery after AMPure bead clean-up
Cause: Beads not resuspended, wrong AXP ratio, or ethanol <70%
→ Always vortex AXP immediately before use. Verify AXP:sample ratio ≥0.4:1. Use freshly prepared 80% ethanol. Check fragment length on agarose gel first.
⚠️ Shorter than expected reads
Cause: DNA fragmented during extraction or library prep
→ Use wide-bore tips. Avoid vortexing — flick/invert instead. Run gel electrophoresis before starting. Review ONT extraction method guidelines.

Sequencing Run Issues

⚠️ Fewer pores at run start vs. Flow Cell Check
Cause: Air bubble introduced during priming, flow cell not seated, or library contaminants blocking pores
→ Draw back 20–30 µl before priming to remove air bubble. Re-seat flow cell firmly. Check library purity. Up to 10% variability is expected.
⚠️ Pore occupancy <40%
Cause: Insufficient library loaded, or no FCT added to priming mix
→ Quantify library before loading (target 10–20 fmol for MinION). Confirm FCT was added to FCF before priming.
⚠️ Pore occupancy ~0% (no sequencing)
Cause: Ethanol used in wash step after adapter ligation (denaturing motor protein), or no tether
→ Use LFB or SFB — never ethanol — for the post-adapter-ligation wash. Ensure FCT was included in priming mix.
⚠️ Large proportion of blue (unavailable) pores
Cause: Contaminants in sample blocking/damaging pores
→ MinKNOW unblocking may clear some. If persistent: perform nuclease flush (EXP-WSH004) or reload cleaner library.
⚠️ Large proportion of light-blue (inactive) pores
Cause: Air bubbles introduced during loading (irreversible damage)
→ Prevention is key. Watch ONT priming/loading video. Always remove air bubble before loading.
⚠️ MinKNOW "Script failed" or temperature error
Cause: Software issue or device in cold/poorly ventilated location
→ Restart computer/MinKNOW. Ensure device is at room temp with good ventilation. Store flow cell at 4°C while resolving. Contact support@nanoporetech.com.
